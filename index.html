How to use the SBC — wiring, keys, and menus v1.4 / v1.1

Power and field wiring

  • Power: 16 VAC @ 100 mA at the AC input pair on the terminal block.
  • RS-485: SmartBob A/B (or +/−) to the RS-485 pair; cable shield to shield when required.
  • Do not start a new measurement while SYSTEM BUSY is on—the console is busy on the bus.
  • Terminal numbering (pin 1 → pin 8) is shown in the wiring overview at the bottom of the page.

Physical keys

  • Keyboard: click a console or its buttons so keys route to that unit — then + − Enter apply to that unit only (shell frames are not tab stops).
  • + — Increment, or choose the option shown with “+” on the display.
  • − — Decrement, or choose the option shown with “−” on the display.
  • ENTER — Confirm or advance when the menu asks for Enter.

Menu map — SBC-A (firmware v1.1)

  • Intro — BinMaster SmartBob splash (faceplate SBC-A), version date, then Press Any Key.
  • MAIN MENU — + Setup; − View/Measure (VIEW MEASURED MENU); ENTER ENABLED BIN MENU.
  • Setup — Units; Set Bin Height uses bins 1–99.
  • View measured — Pick bin, MEASURE?, then a simulated measurement with on-screen status lines.
  • Enabled menu — Optional demo flows (measure-all and enable/disable are simplified).

Menu map — SBC-B (firmware v1.4)

  • Intro — BinMaster splash, v1.4 date line, then Press Any Key.
  • Main Menu — + Setup; − Measure (measurement menu).
  • Setup — Units; Set Bin Height (bins 01–30); ENTER returns to Main Menu.
  • Measurement — Bin select, confirm, then a simulated measurement (requires bin height > 0).

SBC-A vs SBC-B

  • SBC-A — Behaves like firmware v1.1 on this page (up to 99 bins; ENABLED-style menus).
  • SBC-B — Behaves like firmware v1.4 on this page (up to 30 bins; measurement-oriented flow).

Back panel terminal overview

The enclosure sticker lists 16 VAC @ 100 mA. The wiring diagram on the unit labels eight screw terminals left to right (typical labeling): unused positions may be marked NC. Ground reference follows your plant standards; the shield lug is for cable shield drain only.

Troubleshooting

About this webpage

This demo runs only in your browser. It does not connect to sensors or wiring, so it cannot show real communication problems. The notes below describe messages you may see on actual consoles in the field.

No reply from the SmartBob sensor

  • SBC‑B (right) — You usually will not see a short “COMM” code by itself. The display often walks through several lines, for example that the remote did not respond, to check the bin number, and to check RS‑485 wiring. Check for breaks in the wire. Check for SmartBob COM chip failure by testing one address in the daisy chain at a time. Unplug the RS‑485 terminal block on each unit to test for COM failures. Ensure RS‑485 wiring is intact. If you use wireless radios, ensure they are communicating with all four lights (BinMaster wireless radio documentation).
  • SBC‑A (left) — You may see Measurement Aborted together with COMMUNICATION ERROR. If the sensor is in override, the display may say BIN IS IN OVERRIDE instead.

Other messages

  • ERROR: Bin Height=0 — Enter a valid bin height in Setup before measuring.
  • SYSTEM BUSY (LED on) — Wait until the LED turns off before starting another measurement.
  • Override, filling, or Bob stuck — Your plumb bob may be unable to drop because of overfill, stuck in material, or lost in the bin. Follow your site’s procedure and safety rules.

Quick checks

  • Console matches nameplate power (often 16 VAC).
  • RS‑485 pair wired correctly (A/B or +/− per your drawing).
  • Ensure Bin Number matches the Binary Address Dipswitches located on the SmartBob Unit.
  • If screen is not showing with confirmed voltage, or buttons are no longer functional, please contact BinMaster sales to order a new C-100 Unit. The SBC-A and SBC-B are retired units.
  • If you own an SBC-B unit, your SmartBobs must be in jumper Protocol B. If you own an SBC-A unit, your SmartBobs must be in jumper Protocol A. Ensure your SmartBobs are compatible.
